Kevin Liao

Overview

Hi, my name is Kevin Liao. I grew up in a suburb of Denver known as Littleton, Colorado and I am currently a first-year Computer Science student at the Georgia Institute of Technology.

I didn't make my decision to pursue a career in Computer Science until my senior year in high school, when I took AP Computer Science. Before my senior year, I had always thought that I was going to be an engineer. Learning about basic Java and Object Oriented design inspired me to immerse myself in computer science. I realized that I am truly passionate about software and computers and how they interact with the world.

Since my introduction to the world of CS, I have sought to learn new skills on my own time to expand my knowledge and feed my curiosity. For example, I have taken advantage of resources such as Lynda and YouTube to learn basic HTML, Bootstrap, CSS, and iOS Development through Xcode.

As a new recruit to the field of computer science, I have only worked on a small number of projects (you can check them out here!) and my skill set is still expanding. However, I seek to take advantage of the many resources at Georgia Tech and use my time here to continue to build projects and integrate myself with the world of CS.

Georgia Institute of Technology

My 2017 Spring Semester Classes:

CS 1332 - Data Structures and Algorithms (Java)

CS 1332 utilizes Java to cover essential data structures and algorithms in computer science. This spring, I will be learning in-depth about topics ranging from Linked Lists to the KMP (Knuth-Morris-Pratt) String Searching Algorithm (A full list of topics can be found and downloaded here). This class will provide me with a solid foundation for pursuing a career in computer science.

CS 2051 - Honors Discrete Mathematics

CS 2051 is a class dedicated to helping students think differently about mathematics and computer science. Through learning about topics such as graph theory, cryptography, counting, and probability, I hope to be able to think about Computer Science in a more creative and flexible manner. More information about the topics covered in the course can be viewed on the course website here.

Math 2550 - Intro to Multivariable Calculus

Math 2550 is a multivariable calculus class designed specifically for CS students at Georgia Tech. We will be covering topics ranging from partial derivatives to vectors and cross products. This course will help me learn how to apply multivariable calculus concepts to real world problems and to think critically in three dimensions.

Physics 2211 - Intro to Physics I (Modern)

This semester, I will be taking the intro physics course at Gerogia Tech with a modern twist. In addition to studying force and motion, we will also solve problems computationally using a visual module for Python called VPython. This is a cool way to integrate physics concepts and computer programming and it is a great fit for a computer science student like me.

INTA 1110 - Intro to International Relations

I chose an international relations class as my social science elective because the field of computer science continues to have a significant global impact. As an aspiring computer programmer, I am interested in staying up to date on world events and learning about how the world's governments interact with each other and solve problems. I hope that taking this class will help me understand why computer science is so important and how it can have a positive impact on a global scale.
