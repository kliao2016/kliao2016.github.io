A Collection of My Projects

MapView for UWTicketBuddy

❮ ❯

UWTicketBuddy is a project started at HackGT 2016 by me, Brian Glowniak, Daniel Hathcock, and Yu-Lin Yang. It is an app that is meant to streamline the process of buying and selling football tickets at the University of Wisconsin - Madison because football is such a big deal there. At every game, there are those who want to sell a ticket they already purchased and those who want to get their hands on a ticket, but was unable to get on officially. This app creates a place where these people can easily negotiate trades. I worked on the implementation of the Google Maps API and location services, which will help buyers and sellers see who is near them.

Happy Tap

❮ ❯

Happy Tap is a simple iOS application created by Risha Parikh and me at HackGTeeny 2017 in order to gain more practice with using Xcode and Swift. In creating this application, we learned to work with layout constraints, vertical stack views, horizontal stack views, buttons, labels, and timers. The objective of the game is simple: Two players try to see who can tap the screen the most number of times before a timer runs out. In terms of functionality, the app includes 10-second timer and the ability to start and reset the game. I hope to build off the knowledge I gained from creating this app and create more advanced apps in the future.
