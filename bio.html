About Me

Hi, my name is Kevin Liao. I grew up in a suburb of Denver known as Littleton, Colorado and I am currently a first-year Computer Science student at the Georgia Institute of Technology.

I didn't make my decision to pursue a career in Computer Science until my senior year in high school, when I took AP Computer Science. Before my senior year, I had always thought that I was going to be an engineer. Learning about basic Java and Object Oriented design inspired me to immerse myself in computer science. I realized that I am truly passionate about software and computers and how they interact with the world.

Since my introduction to the world of CS, I have sought to learn new skills on my own time to expand my knowledge and feed my curiosity. For example, I have taken advantage of resources such as Lynda to learn basic HTML, Bootstrap, CSS, and iOS Development through Xcode.

As a new recruit to the field of computer science, I have only worked on a small number of projects (you can check them out here!) and my skill set is still expanding. However, I seek to take advantage of the many resources at Georgia Tech and use my time here to continue to build projects and integrate myself with the world of CS.

Professional Interests

Professionally, I am interested in big data, computer networks, information management, operating systems, and embedded computational artifacts. I also enjoy web development and app development. At Georgia Tech, I am considering pursuing a Computer Science degree with threads in Systems and Architecture and Info Internetworks. However, these threads may change as I continue to take classes and refine my interests. Computer science is such a comprehensive field that as a beginner, I want to be open to anything to learn as much as possible and realize what I am most passionate in.
